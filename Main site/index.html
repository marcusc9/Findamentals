Editor’s Brief

Capital is still moving. It is simply becoming more selective.

Energy has reintroduced inflation tension just as markets were hoping for cleaner relief. Growth is still standing, but the easy narrative has broken. What survives now is quality: durable margins, sound balance sheets, credible management, and assets that do not need a perfectly timed policy rescue.

Today’s edition follows that thread across three lenses. The lead read looks at policy credibility and valuation discipline. The skill drill shows how operating leverage can magnify apparently small shifts in demand. The historical lens asks which inflation-era lessons are still useful, and which ones deserve to be left in the archive.
