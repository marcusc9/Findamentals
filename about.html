About Findamentals

A finance platform designed to be read, learned, and remembered.

Traditional financial publications do valuable reporting. Findamentals is trying to solve a different problem: helping readers absorb finance knowledge while they consume the day’s story.

What others do well

  • Fast reporting and sharp opinion
  • Strong scoops and institutional credibility
  • Broad markets and business coverage

What Findamentals adds

  • Interactive, scroll-led reading rather than static article consumption
  • News, skills, history, and research in one sequence
  • Writing that welcomes both learners and professionals

Daily Briefing

One strong entry point, not a cluttered homepage.

The home page is meant to feel editorial rather than feed-like. Readers should understand the day’s most important idea quickly, then choose how deeply they want to go.

People To Watch

Real people are part of the reading layer.

Operators, allocators, and economists sit inside the article experience because they often frame the story as much as the headline itself.

Source Trail

Primary material sits beneath the interpretation.

Research papers, filings, speeches, and public releases are there for readers who want to go beyond the summary and inspect the underlying evidence.

People In The Read

Some belong on your LinkedIn follow list. Some are the story.

Operator

Executives whose commentary reveals the real economy

Demand softness, pricing power, hiring, spending discipline, and funding conditions often appear in management language before they become consensus narratives.

Allocator

Investors who explain how they are framing risk

Good allocators do more than call the market. They make trade-offs visible: liquidity, valuation, duration, and scenario discipline.

Policy voice

Officials and economists who shape the reaction function

When inflation, labour markets, or growth shift, the people interpreting those moves are part of the read, not just background scenery.

Specialist

Experts who notice detail before it becomes mainstream

Credit analysts, market strategists, and sector researchers often spot the small signal long before the rest of the timeline catches up.
