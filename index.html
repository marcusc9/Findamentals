Editor’s Brief

The edition is about when discipline should bend, and when it should not.

The Bank of England is facing a supply shock at the same time as the labour market is losing heat. That is exactly when policy discipline matters most: not because rates must stay high forever, but because the first cut has to arrive with a clear reason.

The same logic carries into personal finance. Your next pound should go where it removes the largest risk-adjusted drag, not where the loudest product sits. Black Wednesday closes the loop: credibility is valuable, but defending the wrong anchor can become very expensive.
