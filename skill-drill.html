How to test private credit fragility without the jargon fog.

High yield is not the same as high quality. This drill turns private-credit risk into four practical checks: cash interest coverage, covenant headroom, maturity walls, and dependence on PIK interest or amend-and-extend relief.

Coverage Covenants Rollover risk
Coverage, headroom, maturity, and refinancing pressure.

Start with cash interest coverage. The simplest version is cash EBITDA divided by cash interest expense. A borrower with GBP 40 million of EBITDA and GBP 18 million of cash interest has 2.2 times coverage. If base rates rise, margins widen, or EBITDA add-backs prove optimistic, that cushion can shrink quickly.

The better version is fixed-charge coverage: cash EBITDA minus maintenance capex, divided by cash interest plus scheduled principal, leases, and other unavoidable charges. That number asks whether the borrower can meet the full cash burden, not just the coupon. If coverage is only protected by aggressive cost add-backs, it is weaker than the headline suggests.

Next, test covenant headroom. If a loan allows net debt / EBITDA of 6.0 times and the borrower is already at 5.4 times, a small EBITDA miss can matter. Loan-to-value gives a second lens: debt divided by enterprise value. A valuation that looked conservative at entry can become thin protection if sector multiples fall before the borrower refinances.

Then map the maturity wall. The rollover gap is the debt due in the next refinancing window minus committed liquidity and realistic free cash flow. A borrower can be current today and fragile tomorrow if most of its debt must be refinanced at a higher spread before earnings have caught up.

Finally, look for dependency on PIK interest or amend-and-extend terms. These tools can be useful bridges, but they can also delay recognition of a borrower that cannot support cash interest or refinance normally. A fund advertising yield should be able to explain marks, borrower concentration, liquidity terms, and how many credits need relief rather than just time.
