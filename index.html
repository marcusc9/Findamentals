Editor's Brief

The edition is about credit risk that looks calm until confidence moves.

Private credit grew by offering borrowers flexible capital and investors floating-rate income, but its strengths also make stress harder to observe. Marks can lag reality, documentation can loosen, semi-liquid vehicles can create redemption tension, and refinancing pressure can build before losses become visible.

The drill gives readers a practical way to test borrower fragility instead of stopping at a headline yield. The historical lens then uses the 1980s junk-bond cycle to separate what is genuinely new about private credit from the old credit-cycle problem of leverage, liquidity confidence, and underwriting discipline.
