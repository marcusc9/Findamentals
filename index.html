Editor's Brief

The edition is about whether the model is measuring discipline or hiding risk.

Microsoft, Amazon, Alphabet, and Meta report in the same window, with AI infrastructure spending now large enough to matter for margins, depreciation, credit markets, and valuation discipline. The question is no longer whether the technology is important. It is whether the incremental economics can carry the capital build-out.

The modelling drill brings that same test down to spreadsheet level: assumptions, checks, scenarios, and audit trails matter because a tidy workbook can still be a bad decision system. The London Whale closes the loop with a reminder that reported risk is only as good as the model, process, and incentives behind it.
